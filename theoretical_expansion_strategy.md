## Priority 1: BEI Passive (被字句) — 3 new sentences recommended

**Why the tree helps most here:** BEI constructions scramble the canonical Agent-Patient word order. The AST tree visually restores this by placing the Agent inside an Embedded Clause governed by 被, making the thematic role inversion visible at a glance.

### Gap 1A: BEI + Resultative Complement
**What's missing:** None of the current BEI sentences pair the passive with a complex resultative complement chain (被…V得…). This is extremely common in spoken Mandarin and creates deep nesting: `被 → Embedded Clause → VP → Head Verb → Complement (resultative)`.  
**Tree value:** ★★★★★ — The tree shows how the complement attaches to the verb *inside* the passive frame, which is invisible in linear text.  
**Example pattern:** 被…骂得很惨 / 被…吓得说不出话

### Gap 1B: BEI + BA double-construction
**What's missing:** Sentences where 被 and 把 co-occur (被他把书拿走了). This is the most structurally complex pattern in Mandarin — a passive wrapping a disposal.  
**Tree value:** ★★★★★ — The tree reveals the double embedding: `被 → Agent → 把 → Patient → VP`. Without the tree, learners cannot parse the role assignments.  
**Example pattern:** 被他把所有的钱都花光了

### Gap 1C: BEI with agentless retained object
**What's missing:** Agentless BEI where the object is "retained" rather than the topic (被偷了手机 where 手机 stays post-verbal). This challenges learners who expect the BEI topic to be the patient.  
**Tree value:** ★★★★ — Shows that the patient is *inside* the VP, not promoted to topic.  
**Example pattern:** 我被偷了手机

## Priority 2: Double Topic (双主题) — 3 new sentences recommended

**Why the tree helps most here:** Double topics create visual nesting that linear parsing cannot reveal. The tree makes the "outer frame → inner comment" hierarchy explicit.

### Gap 2A: Possessor-attribute chain (三层话题)
**What's missing:** A triple-layered topic-comment structure: X, Y, Z-Predicate. We have two-layer (大象/鼻子) but not three-layer.  
**Tree value:** ★★★★★ — The tree visually stacks three Topic nodes, each narrowing the discourse scope. This directly demonstrates the fractal nature of topic-comment.  
**Example pattern:** 那个学校，三年级的学生，数学成绩最好。

### Gap 2B: Contrastive double topic with 倒是
**What's missing:** A double topic where the outer topic sets a contrastive frame using 倒是. This shows topic-comment used for rhetorical contrast, not just description.  
**Tree value:** ★★★★ — Shows the Adjunct 倒是 modifying the inner comment, visually separating "what's being contrasted" from "the surprising twist."  
**Example pattern:** 这个人，脾气倒是不错。

### Gap 2C: Double topic with embedded 是…的 focus
**What's missing:** A double topic where the inner comment is a 是…的 cleft. This cross-pollinates two structural patterns and creates the deepest nesting in the system.  
**Tree value:** ★★★★★ — Shows Topic → Topic → Comment(是…的), demonstrating how Chinese builds arbitrarily deep structures through topic stacking.  
**Example pattern:** 那本书，作者是在北京写的。

## Priority 3: Resultative Complements (结果补语) — 2 new sentences recommended

### Gap 3A: Resultative with quantified retained object
**What's missing:** A resultative where a quantity phrase appears as a retained object after the complement (喝醉了三杯酒). The tree shows the Object sitting *after* the Complement node — violating learners' V-O expectations.  
**Tree value:** ★★★★ — Visually demonstrates the non-standard word order created by complement insertion.  
**Example pattern:** 他喝醉了三杯酒

### Gap 3B: Chained resultative (V1-R1 then V2-R2)
**What's missing:** Two resultative constructions in series within one comment clause. This stresses the tree's ability to show parallel VP structures.  
**Tree value:** ★★★★ — The tree displays two parallel VP branches, each with its own Head Verb + Complement pair.  
**Example pattern:** 她走累了，坐下来休息。

## Priority 4: Potential Complements (可能补语) — 2 new sentences recommended

### Gap 4A: Potential complement in a BEI frame
**What's missing:** A potential complement embedded inside a passive construction (这本书被翻译不了). This is the deepest nesting pattern for complements.  
**Tree value:** ★★★★★ — Tree shows: BEI → Embedded Clause → VP → Head Verb → 不 → Complement. Learners can trace the negation particle's exact position in the stack.  
**Example pattern:** 这种病治不好

### Gap 4B: Potential complement with 来/去 directional
**What's missing:** A potential complement involving a compound directional (V得/不+Direction+来/去). We have simple directional potential (受不了) but not compound.  
**Tree value:** ★★★★ — Shows the three-layer complement stack: `Head Verb → 得/不 → Direction → 来/去`.  
**Example pattern:** 这个箱子搬不上去

## Priority 5: Even (连…都/也) — 2 new sentences recommended

### Gap 5A: 连 with clausal scope + inner topic
**What's missing:** A 连 construction where the emphasized element is an entire clause, not just a noun phrase. We have noun-scope (连自己的名字) but not clause-scope (连他说什么都不知道).  
**Tree value:** ★★★★★ — The tree shows the 连 bracket wrapping an entire Embedded Clause node, with its own internal topic-comment structure. This is the strongest demonstration of recursive nesting.  
**Example pattern:** 她连他叫什么都不记得。

### Gap 5B: Double 连 with contrastive 都/也
**What's missing:** A construction with two 连 phrases in parallel for rhetorical escalation (连A都…，连B也…).  
**Tree value:** ★★★★ — Shows a Parallel Sentence node with two mirror-image 连 structures, demonstrating structural symmetry.  
**Example pattern:** 连名字都没说，连电话也没留。
