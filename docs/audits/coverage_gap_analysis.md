# Structural Coverage Gap Analysis
This report evaluates the 97 sentences currently populating our 19 grammar categories. It identifies structural gaps where a category relies too heavily on identical syntactic patterns and misses fundamental variations required for comprehensive learner acquisition.

---

## 1. Basic Topic-Comment (主题评论)
**Current coverage** (13 sentences): Strong coverage of agent-topics (Subject-Predicate disguised as Topic-Comment), nested serial verbs, and situational topics (e.g. classical idioms).
**Missing patterns**:
1. **Patient-topic sentences**: E.g., `苹果他吃了。` (The apple, he ate.) This is crucial for showing that the Topic doesn't always perform the action, a core tenet of Mandarin.
2. **Time/Location-topic sentences**: E.g., `明天我休息。` or `墙上挂着一幅画。` (Tomorrow I rest / On the wall hangs a picture).
**Priority**: High

## 2. BA Construction (把字句)
**Current coverage** (4 sentences): Excellent coverage of affirmative BA sentences using directional, resultative, and locative complements.
**Missing patterns**:
1. **Negative BA sentences**: E.g., `他没把门关上。` (He didn't close the door) or `别把它弄坏了。` (Don't break it). Learners need to know `没/别/不` must be placed *before* `把`.
2. **BA with retained objects**: E.g., `他把橘子剥了皮。` (He peeled the skin off the orange). High-level but very common pattern.
**Priority**: High

## 3. BEI Passive (被字句)
**Current coverage** (5 sentences): Covers standard agent-specified passives (`被+人/警察/小孩儿+V`).
**Missing patterns**:
1. **Agentless BEI**: E.g., `钱包被偷了。` (The wallet was stolen). Learners must see that naming the agent is optional.
2. **Ràng/Jiào (让/叫) Passives**: E.g., `我的车让人骑走了。` Colloquial speech uses `让` or `叫` far more often than formal `被`.
**Priority**: High

## 4. Shì-de Construction (是…的)
**Current coverage** (5 sentences): Emphasizes time (`昨天晚上`), place (`在上海`), and purpose (`用来...`).
**Missing patterns**:
1. **Pure Means/Manner Focus**: We need an unambiguous example of transportation/method. E.g., `我是坐火车来的。` (I came by train). Note: one current sentence's explanation mentions "by plane", but the Chinese string is actually a mismatch using `昨天`. 
2. **Negative Shì-de**: E.g., `我不是故意打碎的。` (I didn't break it on purpose). Critical to show that the negation `不` goes before `是`.
**Priority**: High

## 5. Comparatives (比较句)
**Current coverage** (5 sentences): Covers `比...adj得多`, `跟...一样`, `像...一样`, and classical equivalents (`不如`).
**Missing patterns**:
1. **Negative comparison (没有)**: E.g., `我没有他高。` (I am not as tall as him). This is the standard way to say "A is less [adjective] than B."
2. **Specific quantity disparities**: E.g., `他比我大三岁。` (He is three years older than me). Showing how numbers slot in.
**Priority**: Medium

## 6. Rhetorical Questions (反问句)
**Current coverage** (4 sentences): Highly concentrated on the `难道...吗` framework.
**Missing patterns**:
1. **不是...吗？ (Isn't it...?)**: E.g., `你不是已经吃过了吗？` (Didn't you already eat?). Much more common in casual conversation than intense `难道`.
2. **怎么/哪能 (How could...)**: E.g., `我怎么知道？` (How would I know?).
**Priority**: Medium

## 7. Separable Verbs (离合词)
**Current coverage** (4 sentences): Good coverage of inserting duration, frequency, and aspect markers (`了`) into classic V-O compounds.
**Missing patterns**:
1. **Question Word Insertion**: E.g., `你生什么气？` (What are you angry about?). This proves to the learner that the object half is functioning as a true noun.
2. **Verb Reduplication**: E.g., `我们去散散步吧。` (Let's go take a stroll). Shows the `AAB` reduplication pattern unique to separable verbs.
**Priority**: Medium

## 8. Resultative Complements (结果补语)
**Current coverage** (5 sentences): Covers `饱`, `懂`, `完`, `红`.
**Missing patterns**:
1. **Negative Past Resultative (没)**: E.g., `我没听懂。` (I didn't understand). Essential to contrast with potential complements.
2. **Phase Complements**: Complements that don't just change state, but confirm completion of a goal (e.g., `找/到`, `做/成`).
**Priority**: Low

## 9. Serial Verb Constructions (连动句)
**Current coverage** (5 sentences): Covers chronological sequence, method-purpose, and location-purpose chains.
**Missing patterns**:
1. **Coverb/Instrumental Chains**: E.g., `他用筷子吃饭。` (He uses chopsticks to eat). Treating instruments as the first verb in the chain.
**Priority**: Low

## 10. Discourse Context (跨句语境)
**Current coverage** (5 sentences): Very strong demonstration of dropped topics and objects over consecutive dialogue turns.
**Missing patterns**:
1. **Dropped Verb / Echo Responses**: E.g., `我也是。` (Me too) or `不。` (No). Showing that Mandarin can even drop the main predicate if the context is rich enough.
**Priority**: Low

## 11. Double Topic (双主题)
**Current coverage** (4 sentences): Covers Whole-Part (`他，眼睛大`) and Frame-Focus.
**Missing patterns**:
1. **Time/Location Inner Topics**: E.g., `中国，明天我不去了。` (As for China, I'm not going tomorrow). Showing time acting as an inner topic.
**Priority**: Low

## 12. Pivotal Constructions (兼语句)
**Current coverage** (5 sentences): Covers `让`, `叫`, `派`, `请`.
**Missing patterns**:
1. **Chained Pivots**: E.g., `老板叫我请他吃饭。` (The boss told me to treat him to a meal). Demonstrating how embedding can go multiple levels deep.
2. **Descriptive Pivots**: E.g., `有个朋友来看我。` (There is a friend coming to see me). Using `有` as the first verb.
**Priority**: Medium

## 13. Potential Complements (可能补语)
**Current coverage** (4 sentences): Covers positive `得` and negative `不` alongside `到`, `完`, and `着`.
**Missing patterns**:
1. **Lexicalized Potential Complements**: E.g., `对不起` (sorry) or `看不起` (look down upon). Learners must realize these standard vocabulary words are structurally potential complements.
2. **Directional Potentials**: E.g., `爬得上去` (can climb up).
**Priority**: Medium

## 14. Degree Complements (程度补语)
**Current coverage** (5 sentences): Covers basic adjectives, intense clauses, and similes (`像...一样`).
**Missing patterns**:
1. **Negative Degree**: E.g., `他跑得不快。` (He doesn't run fast). Demonstrating that the negation goes *after* the `得`.
2. **Inverted Extreme Degree**: E.g., `热死我了` or `高兴极了`. While technically distinct, beginners group these extreme state descriptors together.
**Priority**: Medium

## 15. Directional Complements (趋向补语)
**Current coverage** (4 sentences): Covers compound directionals (`进来`, `出去`, `回来`) and object splitting (`拿回书来`).
**Missing patterns**:
1. **Figurative/Idiomatic Directionals**: E.g., `想起来` (recall to mind), `坚持下去` (keep it up). Highlighting that directional markers often map to abstract metaphorical extensions (time continuing, memories emerging).
**Priority**: High

## 16. Correlative Patterns (越…越…)
**Current coverage** (6 sentences): High focus on `越...越...`, `一...就...`, and `连...都...`.
**Missing patterns**:
1. **Conjunctive Logic Pairs**: Missing basic foundational pairings: `不但…而且…` (not only... but also), `虽然…但是…` (although... but), or `不是…就是…` (if it's not A, it's B).
**Priority**: High

## 17. Aspect Markers (着/过/了)
**Current coverage** (4 sentences): Demonstrates basic use of each marker independently.
**Missing patterns**:
1. **Double LE (了...了)**: E.g., `我学了三年中文了。` (I have studied Chinese for three years [and continue to do so]). Essential for conveying "up to now" vs "in the past".
2. **Simultaneous Action with ZHE**: E.g., `他笑着说。` (He spoke while smiling). Currently we only have stative `着` (`门开着呢`).
**Priority**: High

## 18. Conditional Sentences (如果…就…)
**Current coverage** (6 sentences): Thoroughly covers `如果...就...` and `只要...就...`.
**Missing patterns**:
1. **Concessive Conditionals**: E.g., `即使下雨，我也要去。` (Even if it rains, I'm still going).
2. **Negative Conditionals**: E.g., `除非你请客，否则我不去。` (Unless you treat, I'm not going).
**Priority**: Medium

## 19. Even (连…都/也)
**Current coverage** (4 sentences): Good baseline coverage.
**Missing patterns**:
1. **Extreme Minimization**: E.g., `一点儿也不...` / `一个都不...`. The classical pairing alongside `连` to show absolute negation. 
**Priority**: Low
