# Pedagogical Expansion Strategy

**Author:** Educational Publisher  
**Purpose:** Counter-recommendation to `theoretical_expansion_strategy.md`, evaluating the 12 proposed gaps from a learner's perspective  
**Constraint:** Prioritize structures where the visual AST tree model is *particularly useful* for explaining the grammar

---

## Executive Summary

The Linguistics Specialist's structural analysis is excellent — every gap identified is real, and the tree-value ratings are accurate. However, **learner priorities diverge from linguistic priorities in three important ways**:

1. **Frequency in daily speech** matters more than nesting depth. A 4-level nesting pattern that appears once per 1,000 sentences is less valuable to a learner than a 2-level pattern they'll encounter ten times a day.
2. **Intermediate ceiling patterns** — structures that repeatedly trip up HSK 3-5 learners — deserve priority over advanced-exotic structures that only appear in literary or formal registers.
3. **Incremental complexity** is better pedagogy than maximum complexity. We should add sentences that bridge existing coverage to the proposed deep-nesting sentences, not leap directly to the most extreme cases.

## Gap-by-Gap Pedagogical Evaluation

### Priority 1: BEI Passive (被字句) — 3 proposed → **AGREE: 3, with substitution**

| Gap | Linguist Rating | Pedagogical Rating | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1A: BEI + Resultative | ★★★★★ | ★★★★★ | ✅ **STRONG AGREE** |
| 1B: BEI + BA double | ★★★★★ | ★★★☆☆ | ⚠️ **DEPRIORITIZE** |
| 1C: Agentless retained object | ★★★★ | ★★★★★ | ✅ **UPGRADE PRIORITY** |

**Rationale:**

- **1A (BEI + Resultative):** This is the most important gap in the entire dataset. Sentences like 被骂得很惨 and 被吓得说不出话 are *daily-use Mandarin* that HSK 4 learners encounter constantly. The tree beautifully shows the complement nesting inside the passive frame. **Must-add.**

- **1B (BEI + BA double):** While structurally fascinating, 被他把书拿走了 is **quite rare in natural speech** and actively avoided in formal writing. Most native speakers would say 书被他拿走了 instead. Adding this as one of only 8 BEI examples risks giving learners the impression it's more common than it is. **Recommend replacing with a BEI + 给 variant** (e.g., 书被他给弄丢了), which is far more frequent in spoken Mandarin and still creates interesting tree nesting with the 给-insertion.

- **1C (Agentless retained object):** The linguist rated this ★★★★ but from a learner's perspective this deserves ★★★★★. The pattern 我被偷了手机 is a **classic exam pitfall** — learners constantly struggle with where the patient sits in agentless BEI. The tree makes the structural surprise visible. **Upgrade to top-3 priority.**

### Priority 2: Double Topic (双主题) — 3 proposed → **AGREE: 2, drop 1**

| Gap | Linguist Rating | Pedagogical Rating | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2A: Triple topic chain | ★★★★★ | ★★★☆☆ | ⚠️ **DEPRIORITIZE** |
| 2B: Contrastive 倒是 | ★★★★ | ★★★★☆ | ✅ **AGREE** |
| 2C: Double topic + 是…的 | ★★★★★ | ★★★★★ | ✅ **STRONG AGREE** |

**Rationale:**

- **2A (Triple topic):** A three-layer topic chain like 那个学校，三年级的学生，数学成绩最好 is **grammatically legitimate but stylistically marked**. In practice, native speakers usually break this into two sentences. Adding it risks making double-topic look harder than it is, which could intimidate intermediate learners. **Recommend deferring to a future "Advanced Nesting" expansion.** Instead, add a more natural double topic like the "as for X, regarding Y" pattern with 关于 or 对于, which learners encounter in reading and formal speech daily.

- **2B (Contrastive 倒是):** Good pick. 倒是 is a high-frequency discourse marker that learners hear constantly but struggle to use. The tree clearly shows how it modifies the inner comment. **Keep.**

- **2C (Double topic + 是…的):** Excellent cross-pollination. 那本书，作者是在北京写的 is completely natural, tests two patterns simultaneously, and the tree shows depth-4 nesting. **Keep as high priority.**

### Priority 3: Resultative Complements (结果补语) — 2 proposed → **AGREE: 1, substitute 1**

| Gap | Linguist Rating | Pedagogical Rating | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3A: Quantified retained object | ★★★★ | ★★☆☆☆ | ❌ **DROP** |
| 3B: Chained V-R in series | ★★★★ | ★★★★☆ | ✅ **AGREE** |

**Rationale:**

- **3A (Quantified retained object):** 他喝醉了三杯酒 is **ungrammatical for most native speakers**. The standard phrasing is 他喝了三杯酒喝醉了 (repeating the verb) or just 他喝醉了. A quantified object after a resultative complement is extremely marginal. Adding it would teach learners a pattern that natives would correct them for using. **Drop entirely.**

- **3B (Chained V-R):** 她走累了，坐下来休息 is perfect — natural, frequent, and the tree shows two parallel VP branches with clear head-verb + complement pairs. **Keep.**

- **Substitute 3A with: Resultative + 把 combination.** We have 把 sentences and resultative sentences separately but not combined (把杯子打碎了). This is an HSK 4 staple and the tree shows how 把 frames the patient before the V-R fusion. **High-frequency, high tree-value.**

### Priority 4: Potential Complements (可能补语) — 2 proposed → **AGREE: 2, with note**

| Gap | Linguist Rating | Pedagogical Rating | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| 4A: Potential in BEI frame | ★★★★★ | ★★★★☆ | ✅ **AGREE (with simpler example)** |
| 4B: Compound directional potential | ★★★★ | ★★★★★ | ✅ **UPGRADE** |

**Rationale:**

- **4A (Potential in BEI):** The concept is valid but the proposed example 这种病治不好 is actually **not a BEI sentence** — there's no 被. A true BEI + potential would be 这道题被他做不出来, which is quite stiff and formal. **Recommend a standalone V-不-R sentence** (这种病治不好) as a regular potential complement instead, since it fills a different gap: the "subjectless potential" pattern where the topic is the patient, not the agent. The tree shows this role inversion cleanly.

- **4B (Compound directional potential):** 这个箱子搬不上去 is **incredibly useful**. Compound directional potentials are HSK 4 bread-and-butter and the three-layer stack (V → 不 → Direction → 来/去) is exactly the kind of dense structure learners cannot parse without the tree. **Upgrade to top priority in this category.**

### Priority 5: Even (连…都/也) — 2 proposed → **AGREE: 2**

| Gap | Linguist Rating | Pedagogical Rating | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| 5A: Clausal scope 连 | ★★★★★ | ★★★★★ | ✅ **STRONG AGREE** |
| 5B: Double 连 parallel | ★★★★ | ★★★☆☆ | ⚠️ **KEEP, but lower priority** |

**Rationale:**

- **5A (Clausal scope):** 她连他叫什么都不记得 is a perfect addition. The tree showing 连 wrapping an entire embedded clause is the single best demonstration of recursive nesting in the dataset. Learners will have an "aha!" moment. **Must-add.**

- **5B (Double 连 parallel):** 连名字都没说，连电话也没留 is rhetorically elegant but somewhat literary. In everyday speech, people more often use a single 连…都 and let the listener infer the escalation. **Keep as a lower-priority "nice to have."**

## Key Pedagogical Principles Applied

1. **"Would a tutor teach this?"** — Every sentence we add should be something a private Mandarin tutor would naturally introduce at HSK 3-5 level. If a tutor would say "you don't really need this yet," it should be deferred.

2. **"Can the learner use it tomorrow?"** — Patterns that appear in daily conversation, WeChat messages, or TV dramas get priority over patterns that only appear in literary essays or linguistics papers.

3. **"Does the tree earn its keep?"** — We agree with the Linguistics Specialist's guiding principle. But among high-tree-value patterns, we should still prefer the ones learners will actually encounter.

4. **"Don't scare the intermediate learner."** — The jump from our current coverage to BEI+BA double construction is too steep. Build bridges (BEI+给, BEI+Resultative) before introducing the most extreme nesting patterns.
